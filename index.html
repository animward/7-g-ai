7G Principles

Temporal Sovereignty

Decisions today must serve the seventh generation. Indigenous temporal thinking applied to technology roadmaps.

Regenerative Systems

Systems that regenerate rather than extract. Technology modeled on living ecosystems, not industrial machines.

Knowledge Persistence

Knowledge that persists across centuries. Memory systems designed for civilizational time scales.

Ethical Architecture

Ethics embedded in architecture, not bolted on. If the structure is wrong, no amount of governance fixes it.

The Roadmap to 2175

Seven generations is approximately 150 years. The 7G framework asks: what would technology look like if we designed it for 2175 instead of next quarter?

This is not utopian thinking. It is the oldest planning framework on Turtle Island — the Seventh Generation Principle from Haudenosaunee (Iroquois) governance, applied to AI systems, data sovereignty, and computational infrastructure.
