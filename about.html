About 7G

The Seventh Generation Principle originates from the Haudenosaunee (Iroquois) Confederacy — one of the oldest continuously operating democratic governance systems on Earth. The principle states: in every deliberation, consider the impact on the seventh generation yet to come. Seven generations is approximately 150 years.

7G applies this principle to artificial intelligence. The question is not "what can AI do this quarter?" The question is: "what will the AI systems we build today look like to the people living in 2175?"

7G Principles:

  • Temporal Sovereignty — Decisions about technology architecture must account for multi-generational impact. Systems designed for quarterly metrics produce quarterly thinking. Systems designed for civilizational time scales produce civilizational thinking.
  • Regenerative Systems — Technology modeled on living ecosystems rather than industrial machines. Systems that regenerate rather than extract — that leave the computational environment healthier than they found it.
  • Knowledge Persistence — Memory systems designed for centuries, not product cycles. How does knowledge survive platform migrations, format obsolescence, and institutional collapse? The birchbark scrolls survived longer than any database.
  • Ethical Architecture — Ethics embedded in system architecture from the foundation. Not bolted on after the fact. Not in a governance committee. In the code. If the structure is wrong, no amount of governance fixes it.

7G is not utopian thinking. It is the oldest planning framework on Turtle Island, applied to the newest technology humanity has produced. Indigenous temporal thinking is not a metaphor — it is an engineering methodology.
